I am happy with the tools I used, but I did feel a bit restricted at times.

Lack of PHP knowledge for contact form

When attempting to create my contact form, I could only get so far with HTML and CSS. If I had been able to use PHP for this, it would have made my form fully functional.

Trello

I also found Trello useful. At the beginning of my project, I didn't realise that there was a due date reminder. However, halfway through my project, I had a closer look at Trello to check if there were any good features I was missing, and then found this feature. I found it useful, so I used it to measure my deadlines at a crucial point in my project - when it was nearing an end.

Padlet

Padlet became more and more useful for me as the project progressed. The reason for this is because the ratings system made me realise early on that I was reading too many articles that had irrelevant content. Mapping out my research on Padlet and seeing which types of articles I was giving 4 and 5 stars allowed me to recognise what kinds of articles I find more useful. This ended up being articles in short, concise bullet points rather than pages and pages of useless information.
